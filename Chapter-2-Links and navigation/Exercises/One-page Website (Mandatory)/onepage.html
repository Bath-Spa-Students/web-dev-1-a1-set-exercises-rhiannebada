SERIES

VIOLET EVERGARDEN


▶ PREVIEW



OVERVIEW

After four long years of conflict, The Great War has finally come to an end. Caught up in the bloodshed was Violet Evergarden, a young girl raised to be a deadly weapon on the battlefield. Hospitalized and maimed in a bloody skirmish during the War's final leg, she was left with only words from the person she held dearest, but no understanding of their meaning. Recovering from her wounds, Violet starts a new life working at CH Postal Services. There, she witnesses by pure chance the work of an "Auto Memory Doll", amanuenses that transcribe people's thoughts and feelings into words on paper. Moved by the notion, Violet begins work as an Auto Memory Doll, a trade that will take her on an adventure, one that will reshape the lives of her clients and hopefully lead to self-discovery.

CAST : Yui Ishikawa, Takehito Koyasu, Daisuke Namikawa, Aya Endo, Koki Uchiyama, Minori Chihara, Haruka Tomatsu

GENRES : Anime, Romance, Drama, Fantasy

THIS SHOW IS : Intimiate, Heartfelt, Emotional

MATURITY RATING : 16+ | SUBSTANCES | Recommended for ages 16 and up


MAIN CHARACTERS | CAST

VIOLET EVERGARDEN (ヴァイオレット・エヴァーガーデン)

Voiced by: Yui Ishikawa

Violet is a newcomer to the CH Postal Company, and works as an Auto Memory Doll—a ghostwriter for people who cannot write or are looking for help expressing their emotions in letters.

GILBERT BOUGAINVILLEA (ギルベルト・ブーゲンビリア)

Voiced by: Daisuke Namikawa

A major in the Leidenschaftlich Army, Gilbert came from an aristocratic family. Although he treasures Violet much more than anything else, he never expressed his feelings to her because of their identity as soldiers.

CLAUDIA HODGINS (クラウディア・ホッジンズ)

Voiced by: Takehito Koyasu

A former army lieutenant colonel, Claudia is the president of the CH Postal Company. He was a good friend of Gilbert during their time in the military and Claudia attempts to help Violet take the first steps of her new life.

CATTLEYA BAUDELAIRE (カトレア・ボードレール)

Voiced by: Aya Endō

Cattleya is an Auto Memory Doll who works alongside Violet at the CH Postal Company as its most popular Doll. She has been close to Hodgins since before the company was established, and joined as one of its initial employees.

BENEDICT BLUE (ベネディクト・ブルー)

Voiced by: Koki Uchiyama

Benedict is a eccentric postman who works at the CH Postal Company. Alongside Cattleya, he maintained a close relationship with Hodgins before the company's establishment and eventually became one of its initial employees.

ERICA BROWN (エリカ・ブラウン)

Voiced by: Minori Chihara

Erica is an Auto Memory Doll working at the CH Postal Company. Her skills when it comes to client interaction are poor, but she continues to believe in her work as a Doll.

IRIS CANNARY (アイリス・カナリー)

Voiced by: Haruka Tomatsu

Iris is an Auto Memory Doll who started working at the CH Postal Company shortly after Erica was hired. Hailing from the small farming village of Kazaly, Iris has always admired the image of a working woman.

This anime is one of the best shows I've seen, and it's honestly one of my favorites. It's such a sad yet beautiful one. I had never been so fascinated with writing letters before seeing this, and man, this show is just so good that it got me to care about letters so much and learn the impact they could have on people. It's a stunning show, the animation is excellent, and there are some amazing and beautiful shots/scenes throughout. The show is about this girl named Violet who is ex-military and after the last big battle that happened her commander told her that he “loved her” and ever since then she goes on this amazing journey trying to find out the meaning of “I love you.” With each episode, Violet grows, and her story is quite beautiful, and it's just amazing to see it all unfold. She made many friends, some who helped her on her journey, and she helped many people. The soundtrack is something else, and I listen to it all the time. I loved Luculia and Violet's friendship, and I wish we got to see more of them, maybe even working together on a project, but I’m still happy with what we got. I don't get very emotional while watching movies/shows, but this one had me tearing up quite a bit. From what I understand, the manga is pretty different from the series, but I prefer the anime for most of the changes. Anyways this is a great show, and I HIGHLY recommend it!

Violet Evergarden is an anime series from 2018 that focuses to the titular character Violet, a war veteran who know nothing about normal life and works as a ghostwriter to find the meaning of feeling and affection which she struggles to understand. This series is definitely one of the best anime drama series of all time. It's one of the few series that's able to perfectly condense a lot of it's side character development and emotional moments in nearly each of the mostly standalone episodes this series has, while slowly building up the main character development whom, by using other people thought and feelings began to understand them and herself. Soundtrack is amazing and mostly suited to the series theme and scenes, although it can be a bit repetitive at times. Graphics and animation are astonishingly beautiful, have a production quality that's on par with many anime theatrical movies, setting a high bar for other anime series to follow up on. While on the other hand character development and emotional buildup is the strong point of the series, it also serves as a minor weakness as some side character that are introduced have too little development while others properly developed and mature enough to have the audience heavily invested in their story. Overall this is a great and must to watch for everyone who love anime and drama adaptations. Beware if you are a crybaby :D
